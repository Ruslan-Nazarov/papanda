# About papanda

## 1. The Breakdown of the Old Concept of Knowledge

With the advent of AI, the very concept of knowledge is undergoing a radical change. More precisely, it is not changing so much as exposing the fact that the dominant concept is losing its meaning.

The essence of this concept was positivistic: to accumulate knowledge, to assemble it into building blocks, and from these blocks to construct a "temple of science." The priests of this temple—scientists—knew its stones to the extent of their memory, training it with endless tasks and exercises. Such a model of knowledge was well described by Feynman's favorite principle: "Shut up and calculate."

Now that AI is taking over these human capabilities, it is destroying the old approach and stripping the priests of their authority. Both the information blocks and their priests are becoming increasingly obsolete. The need for understanding, rather than the mindless accumulation of knowledge, arises much more acutely now.

Among programmers, designers, and lawyers, these sensations are the strongest, accompanied by actual layoffs. Their work, no matter how it might seem from the outside, often had a routine and repetitive nature, largely generalized and consisting of high-level abstractions—exactly what AI picks up so well.

Mathematicians and physicists are also affected, though differently. Their work, although consisting of high-level abstractions, remains somewhat creative—because it comes from objective reality. Unlike programmers, designers, and lawyers, mathematicians and physicists are forced to understand the world that surrounds them. When the world refuses to "bend" to their theories, they are forced to create new concepts and develop old ones.

## 2. The Search for a New Model of Knowledge

Thus, understanding is required. Can we demand it from AI? We can, but this demand will remain unanswered. AI is incapable of understanding, as it goes far beyond elementary division into 0 and 1, the separation of objects, and counting them.

Positivism has hit a wall with AI. Memorizing textbooks is useless: any knowledge today is just one click away. If previously "understanding" meant adding a few more blocks of knowledge, this simply will not work anymore. Understanding movement is something much more than simply knowing what a derivative is. What is needed, then? **Dialectics**—the very dialectics that evolved from Hegel to Marx.

Dialectics is the science of development, of movement. It is not about knowledge being static blocks of information, but about how the world itself develops—the world from which these arrays are subsequently extracted. Most importantly, dialectics works with **contradictions**. The world is contradictory, movement proceeds through contradictions—this is the essence. And it is precisely this that AI is incapable of working with.

The essence is simple: AI has made the transition from the old model of knowledge to a dialectical one inevitable. Only on this path is the further development of AI possible, and only here can the proper place for humanity be found—the leading place. Only a human being can comprehend this world, whereas AI will remain a reliable assistant.

## 3. Educational Applications of the Future

This approach must be embodied in an application for those who need it most—schoolchildren and students. They are the ones currently at the greatest risk of making a mistake in choosing a profession. If one chooses programming, which in 5–10 years will be completely different, one might end up without a job after university. However, by mastering the correct approach, a student will be able to engage in programming on a fundamental level.

An application oriented precisely toward the dialectical assimilation of knowledge is necessary—one that combines all the tools a student needs: from time management to note-taking.

**Note-taking** becomes especially important here. AI can select facts, retell a story, and even invent a solution. But AI is incapable of the most important thing—understanding. One must treat notes as a tool that educates and preserves knowledge: it educates during the process of their creation, and it preserves by calmly waiting in the application for the moment when the student needs to refer to them.

Thus, we need an application that unites all the necessary tools for a student, specifically designed to teach them a dialectical approach to this world. That is the goal. And this is exactly why the papanda application was created.

## 4. Implementing the New Approach in the papanda Application

The papanda application features three main modules: time management, foreign language learning, and notes.

**Time management** contains all the familiar mechanisms of planning and control, but with enhancements that bring it closer to dialectics. The calendar, tasks, and habit tracker utilize stickers that allow for quick notes aimed at conceptualizing the process.

**The foreign language learning module** is located on the same page as the time management tools. Language learning is built upon the dialectical method described in the book "How to Learn Many Foreign Languages? A Dialectical Approach." The essence of the method lies in mastering the structure of sentences—understanding how a sentence "grows" from the predicate to the subject and beyond.

**The core component of the application is notes.** A separate page is dedicated to them. The main space is allocated to a canvas divided into two parts. On the left and the right, one can form blocks containing a dialectical exposition of the subject: the simplest component is taken, the contradiction in which it participates is identified, and then the leap through which this contradiction is resolved is found. The blocks can contain graphs, geometric figures, and mathematical formulas.

Two critical tools are utilized in the notes: a **search for opposites** and a **mathematical formula parser**. AI finds opposites and passes them to the student, who then checks them for contradictions and looks for ways to resolve them. The formula parser explains not how a formula is applied, but how its emergence became possible in the first place—which is exactly what students lack when struggling with mathematics and physics.

## 5. Conclusion

The future of education is quite certain—there must be a shift toward teaching understanding rather than memorization. The majority of educational technologies, programs, approaches, textbooks, and curricula are aimed at the "transfer of knowledge." Knowledge can be transferred, but understanding cannot—it can only be taught.
